Trauma-Informed Notes

  • Calm, non-triggering palette supports emotional safety.
  • All colors meet accessibility contrast standards.
  • Color is used to support—not overwhelm—content hierarchy.
  • Gentle color cues reinforce predictability and user control.

Trauma-Informed Typography & Color Guidelines

  • Always maintain clear headline and body hierarchy for orientation and comfort.
  • Use color to gently reinforce structure, not to create pressure or urgency.
  • Ensure all text and color combinations meet or exceed WCAG AA contrast standards.
  • Favor calm, predictable color and type patterns to reduce cognitive load.
  • Pair Playfair Display (headlines) with Montserrat (body) for clarity and stability.
  • Use trauma-safe spacing and avoid crowding to support psychological safety.
  • Provide content warnings and emergency exit options where appropriate.
  • Test with diverse users, including trauma survivors, for validation.
