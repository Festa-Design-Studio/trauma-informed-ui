Usage Guidelines

- Use semantic colors consistently to maintain clear meaning.

- Ensure sufficient contrast for accessibility (WCAG 2.1 AA).

- Use lighter shades for backgrounds, darker for text.

- Maintain color harmony by using complementary colors.

Calm Gradient Colors

Selenite Gradient

Class: bg-gradient-selenite
Colors: selenite-white-50 ‚Üí selenite-white-100
Direction: Left to right

Usage: Use for backgrounds, cards, and sections to create a soft, calming foundation.

Trauma-Informed Considerations: Gentle, low-contrast gradient reduces visual stress and supports a sense of safety.

Chrysocolla-Azurite Gradient

Class: bg-gradient-chrysocolla-azurite
Colors: chrysocolla-green-50 ‚Üí azurite-blue-50
Direction: Left to right

Usage: Use for hero sections, banners, or to visually separate content areas with a calm, cool transition.

Trauma-Informed Considerations: Combines two calming color families for a sense of trust and tranquility.

Rhodochrosite-Selenite Gradient

Class: bg-gradient-rhodochrosite-selenite
Colors: rhodochrosite-rose-50 ‚Üí selenite-white-50
Direction: Top to bottom

Usage: Use for overlays, cards, or subtle section backgrounds to add warmth without overwhelming.

Trauma-Informed Considerations: Gentle pink-to-white transition offers a sense of gentle urgency and comfort.

Chrysocolla Med Gradient

Class: bg-gradient-chrysocolla-med
Colors: chrysocolla-green-200 ‚Üí chrysocolla-green-400
Direction: Left to right

Usage: Use for callouts, highlights, or to add a sense of growth and renewal.

Trauma-Informed Considerations: Cool green tones support feelings of growth, safety, and calm.
